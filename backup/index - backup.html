Our Tapestry of Tradition

Since 2015, Chronicle of Cloth has been dedicated to preserving the ancient art of textile weaving. Based in Bengaluru, India, we blend traditional craftsmanship with modern elegance, creating timeless pieces that tell stories of heritage and culture.

Our Heritage

Founded by textile enthusiasts with deep roots in India's weaving communities, Chronicle of Cloth began as a small workshop collaborating with five master weavers. Today, we partner with over 200 artisans across Karnataka, Tamil Nadu, and West Bengal, preserving techniques that date back centuries.

Our Mission

We are committed to safeguarding India's textile heritage while creating sustainable livelihoods for rural artisans. Each piece in our collection represents hours of meticulous handwork, using techniques passed down through generations. We believe in fair trade practices and environmentally conscious production methods.

Our Craft

Our artisans specialize in traditional techniques including ikat, jamdani, and kantha embroidery. We source natural fibers and dyes, honoring ancient wisdom while embracing innovation. Every thread tells a story—of heritage, of resilience, of beauty. Our garments bridge the gap between timeless tradition and contemporary design.
